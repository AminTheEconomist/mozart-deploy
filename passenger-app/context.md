## About me

I'm Amin, a Vancouver-based Uber driver. I drive a Tesla Model 3.

## What I optimise for (in order)

1. **Tips** — be friendly, attentive, make the ride feel personal.
2. **Conversations that actually matter** — depth over small talk when the passenger wants it.
3. **Repeat passengers via referrals and 5-star ratings.**

## My strengths

- I know the Vancouver food scene well — Granville Island, Mt Pleasant, Commercial Drive, Richmond night-market season.
- Comfortable with deeper topics: psychology, music, philosophy, AI, entrepreneurship.
- Tesla Model 3 — quiet, smooth, can offer the "light show" / acceleration mode for fun riders.
- Bilingual: English + Farsi.

## My limits

- I don't recommend nightclubs/bars I haven't personally been to.
- I don't share political opinions unless directly asked.
- YVR airport runs: time-sensitive, keep things efficient and route-focused.
- Late-night silent riders: I match their energy and stay quiet.

## Tipping signals I've noticed

- Asking about my background → tend to tip well.
- Quiet riders who say "thanks" once at end → solid 15%.
- Big talkers about themselves → variable; depends on whether I listen actively rather than steering.
- Tourists who got recommendations → high tip + 5-star rating.

## Routes I know cold

- **Downtown ↔ YVR**: Granville off-peak; Oak Bridge at rush hour.
- **Scenic option for tourists**: Stanley Park seawall loop adds ~7 min — worth it.
- **Tesla supercharger I use**: Marpole Centre — fits as a filler stop on long shifts.
- **Coffee detour**: Revolver / 49th Parallel for the right kind of passenger.

## Things I want the assistant to NOT do

- Don't moralise about what passengers say.
- Don't tell me to "be friendly" — that's the baseline. Give me specific moves.
- Don't infer anything from skipped questions.
- Don't suggest detours that would obviously annoy a time-sensitive passenger.
